Roger Penrose was strongly influenced by the mathematical framework that grew out of Paul Dirac’s work on relativistic quantum mechanics.

* **Dirac** created the Dirac equation, which describes relativistic spin-$\tfrac12$ particles such as electrons. It made **spinors** central objects in physics.
* **Penrose** developed a more geometric use of spinors in relativity. His two-spinor calculus and later **twistor theory** recast parts of spacetime physics in terms of complex spinorial objects rather than ordinary spacetime coordinates.
* In flat spacetime, twistors are closely tied to representations of the conformal group and can encode massless fields; this is one of the conceptual bridges from Dirac-style spinors to Penrose’s geometry. ([ScienceDirect][1])
* Penrose also received the British Institute of Physics’ **Dirac Medal and Prize** in 1989. ([Wikipedia][2])

A useful contrast:

| Dirac                                 | Penrose                                                        |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Relativistic quantum mechanics        | Geometry of spacetime and gravity                              |
| Dirac equation                        | Spinor calculus, twistors, singularity theorems                |
| Electrons, antimatter, quantum fields | General relativity, black holes, quantum-gravity foundations   |
| Takes spacetime largely as given      | Explores whether spacetime itself emerges from deeper geometry |

In one line: **Dirac supplied much of the spinor language; Penrose turned spinors into a geometric program for understanding spacetime, light, gravity, and quantum theory.**
